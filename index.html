imaging interaction & telemonitoring | BIMLab research project

I worked as an undergraduate research assistant at the Bodies in Motion Lab at UMBC's Interactive Systems Research Center (Human-Centered Computing department). Under Dr. Helena Mentis and PhD student Azin Semsar, I investigated telemonitoring and remote instructional communication in medical training. My responsibilities as a research assistant include transcribing, coding, and analyzing different types of data including video recordings, physiological signals, eye tracking data, questionnaires, and interviews with medical trainers and trainees. The data was analyzed through statistical analysis, as well as assisting in carrying out field studies.

do you know me | multiplayer game application

Do You Know Me? is a fun, friend-group focused, web-based multiplayer game built around the idea of figuring out how well you know your friends. You can create a game, invite all your friends to join by sending them a game code, choose the question pack you want to play with, and choose how many rounds of the game you‚Äôd like to play. Each round, all players are asked the same question from the pre-chosen game pack and must submit an answer. Afterwards, all the answers are gathered and then all of the players must guess who wrote each answer. For each question and answer pair you match correctly, you get a point. The main idea of the game is to see whether or not you really know your friends!

perception | hackathon for social justice project

I designed the grand-prize winning mobile application Perception that functioned as an interactive learning tool to be used by hiring managers and hiring committees. Its purpose was to combat implicit bias in hiring and recruitment. Within the app, users would be able to take part in virtual workplace simulations and make decisions based on the situations they were put into. After completion, the users would get feedback on the decisions they made and whether or not implicit biases were detected in their choices. This app was created to provide hiring committees with a fun and interactive way to diversify their workforce and help facilitate a more inclusive hiring process.¬†
